Serve6Dots

Serve6Dots is a first of its kind campaign that aims to make Braille and large print menus universally available in restaurants and coffee shops. The campaign focuses on sensitizing the food and beverage industry, and by extension the general public, to challenges the blind and visually impaired face.

The campaign is built on ideas of inclusion, accessibility, and self-reliance to empower visually challenged people to exercise their choices independently. The name Serve6Dots is inspired by the Braille Alphabet, in which each letter is represented by patterns of six raised dots. The word 'serve' has significance in the restaurant industry. It also has a broader significance, referring to ber served and serving others.

We believe in developing solutions that uphold these values, in partnership with Corporates medical professionals and blind and visually challenged individuals. To this end, the project is an official partner of National Braille Press, Boston (United States) and L.V. Prasad Eye Institute, Hyderabad (India).
